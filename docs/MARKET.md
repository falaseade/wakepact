# Market & Competitors

## Competitor table

| App | Platform(s) | Strengths | Gaps we exploit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Alarmy | Android, iOS | Category leader for wake-proof missions (photo, QR, **steps**, math, squats); huge sound library | Entirely solo — you grade your own homework; no human accountability, missions can be turned off in a groggy haze |
| Galarm | Android, iOS | Mature social alarms/reminders; participants are alerted if you don't respond; flexible repetition | Buddies are *notified*, never *empowered* — no wake-proof, no remote off-switch, dismissal is a normal tap |
| Wakie | Android, iOS | Strangers phone you at wake time; novel, genuinely rousing; anonymous calls | Strangers, not your circle → zero lasting accountability; a call can be answered and slept through; no proof of being up |
| WakeMe / YouUp | iOS-centric | Friends record audio/video that *becomes* your alarm sound; warm, personal | Pleasant sound ≠ being awake; nothing verifies you got up; friend involvement is the night before, not the morning of |
| Sleep as Android | Android | Sleep tracking + CAPTCHA/QR dismissals; deep alarm reliability know-how | Solo verification again; complex product where alarms are one feature among many |
| Google Clock / OEM clocks | Android | Pre-installed, reliable, free | Trivially dismissable; the snooze button is the product we are attacking |

## Differentiation statement
Every incumbent picks one half of the problem: Alarmy-style apps verify wakefulness but let the sleeper verify *themselves*; Galarm/Wakie/YouUp-style apps add humans but verify *nothing*. WakePact fuses the halves — a physically demanding, sensor-detected proof **plus** an off-switch that lives in a friend's pocket. The sleeper cannot end the alarm alone (until a deliberately humane timeout), and the friend group sees every outcome in a shared feed. The accountability loop, not the alarm sound, is the product.
